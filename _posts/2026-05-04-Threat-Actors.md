# Introduction
While I have learn previously about attack capabilities, I have never learned about the
actual categorisation of threat actors, we will explore them today.

A threat actor is any entity that causes a security event. Understanding who is attacking
and why helps to tailor defences. Threat actors are categorised by:
- Location
- Sophistication
- Resources
- Motivation

#### Nation State
It is also known as an Advanced Persistent Threat (APT). Backed by govt resources, can run
simultaneous and sustained attacked against multiple targets. It targets for a long term 
access and not a quick smash and grab.

For example, Stuxnet worm by US and Israel targetting Iranian nuclear centrifuges.

#### Unskilled Attacker (Script Kiddie)
Runs scripts or tools without understanding how they work. mostly external and very noob.

#### Hacktivist
PRO version of Unskilled Attacker with common attack types of DoS, website defacement and data
leaks.

#### Insider Threat
ALready has legitimate access internally, hardest to detect and stop. Skill varies. The attack
surface is not the perimeter but the internal trust

#### Organised Crime
Cybercrime as a business with proper departments (hackers, data sellers). Aim for financial profit.

#### Shadow IT
Not really malicious by intent but creates own infrastructure and apps motivated by convenience and
speed. Causes unsecured cloud services, unknown data stores and unpatched systems
