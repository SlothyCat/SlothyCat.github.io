## Compliance
There are some key compliance regulations to take note of:

|Regulation|Area|Coverage|
|---|---|---|
|**SOX** (Sarbanes-Oxley Act 2002)|Finance|Financial data protection and availability for public companies|
|**HIPAA**|Healthcare|Protects private medical information in the US|
|**GLBA** (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act 1999)|Finance|Requires US financial institutions to explain data sharing and protect customer data|

For **HIPAA**, non-compliance would have various types and its respective fine and prison sentence.

| Violation Type                           | Fine                                                                                             | Prison                         |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------ |
| Standard violation                       | Up to $50,000                                                                                    | Up to 1 year (Class 6 Felony)  |
| Under false pretences                    | Up to $100,000                                                                                   | Up to 5 years (Class 5 Felony) |
| Intent to sell/use data for gain or harm | Up to $250,000                                                                                   | Up to 10 years                 |
| Civil fines                              | $100 per violation. Max $25,000 for identical violations during a calendar year (Class 3 Felony) |                                |

> Other consequences could include: Reputational Damage, Licence Loss, Sanctions and Contract Breach

#### Compliance Roles

| Role                               | Responsibility                                                                                                              |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **CCO (Chief Compliance Officer)** | Responsible for organisation-wide compliance with all applicable laws and regulations; reports compliance status internally |
| **Executive attestation**          | Executive signs off confirming compliance is in good standing — responsible for doing so in good faith                      |

#### Difference between Due Diligence and Due Care

|Term|Context|
|---|---|
|**Due care**|Internal activities performed in good faith to maintain compliance|
|**Due diligence**|External activities performed with third parties to verify compliance|

#### Compliance Monitoring
- Internal tools track all compliance tasks and statuses
- May require third-party interaction to verify compliance
- Large Organisations automate as much as possible
- Automated compliance monitoring systems collect data from people, third parties and internal systems
- Compile reports to maintain an up-to-date compliance status

## Privacy
Privacy laws aim to protect personal data. Here are some privacy law levels

|Level|Examples|
|---|---|
|**Local / state**|Records on homes, vehicles, medical licensing|
|**National**|HIPAA (US healthcare privacy)|
|**International**|GDPR (EU — affects multiple countries simultaneously)|

#### GDPR - General Data Protection Regulation
EU regulation protecting privacy for everyone living in the EU.

**Protected data types include:**
- Name, address, photo
- Email details, bank information
- Online social media posts
- Location data, genetic makeup
- And much more

The **Principles**:
1. Data Subject Control - individuals decide what happens with their personal data
2. Right to be Forgotten - can remove their personal data
3. Data Subject Definition - any personal data that can identify someone

#### Data Roles in Privacy Context

|Role|Privacy Responsibility|
|---|---|
|**Data owner**|Overall accountability for a data category (e.g. VP of Sales owns customer relationship data)|
|**Data controller**|Manages how data is used; defines the rules for data processing|
|**Data processor**|Actually uses or processes the data per the controller’s instructions — may be internal or a third party|
Similar to what we have learned.

#### Data Inventory
**A listing of all data an organisation collects and stores**. We also decide the data owner, update frequency and data format.

**Internal uses** of the data inventory include:
- Cross-project collaboration
- IT security assessments
- Data quality checks

When sharing data with **external parties**:
- Must follow all applicable privacy laws
- Requires understanding what data is held and its classification
- Data sharing must fall within existing legal and regulatory guidelines\
