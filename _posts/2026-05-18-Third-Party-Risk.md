# Introduction to Third-Party Risk Assessment
We have assessed a lot of risks related to ourselves, but we have yet to review the risks of vendors and other third parties that are involved with our operations. It is time to finally look at them.

## Third Party Risk Assessment
We need to have a risk assessment of the vendor before entering the relationship. This should be documented in the contract to set expectations and penalties for the breach.

#### Penetration Testing as Risk Assessment
Penetration Testing can be a **contractual requirement** for vendors, and both parties might use the same third-party pen-testing firm. This is governed by the **rules of engagement**, defining scope.
- Test type (On-site, internal, external)
- Timing
- Scope (IP, devices)
- Emergency Contacts
- Sensitive data handling

#### Right to Audit
This is a clause written into the vendor contracts that gives the **right to perform regular security audits of the vendor**. This is used as **evidence of internal audits**. Similar to Rules of Engagement, we need to set parameters for how audits are conducted. This might be mandatory due to compliance.

This is often performed by a neutral third party outside of the contract.

#### Support Chain Analysis
This evaluates the security across the **entire process from raw materials to final product delivery**.

#### Independent Assessments
We can even hire **3rd party assessors who have broader perspectives** from their experience gained from working across many organisations. They would be able to:
1. Identify blind spots that internal teams did not catch
2. Provide insights not available from within the organisation
3. Valuable for both initial and ongoing assessments

#### Due Diligence
We need to carry out our own due diligence to **investigate the third party before doing business** with them. We should verify their claims, conduct background checks and review for any potential conflict of interest.

#### Ongoing Vendor Monitoring
We should continue to monitor our vendors. There are different ways for us to monitor:

|Monitoring Type|Detail|
|---|---|
|Financial health checks|Ensure vendor remains financially stable|
|IT security reviews|Confirm security controls remain in place|
|News and social media monitoring|Watch for public issues with the vendor|
|Questionnaires|Ask vendors about due diligence, disaster recovery, data storage, and protection methods|

## Agreement Types
There are multiple agreements that will be discussed with the vendors.

| Term       | Definition                                                                                       |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **SLA**    | Service Level Agreement — minimum service requirements from a provider                           |
| **MOU**    | Memorandum of Understanding — informal, broad goals, not legally binding                         |
| **MOA**    | Memorandum of Agreement — more detailed than MOU, partially binding                              |
| **MSA**    | Master Service Agreement — legal contract framework for ongoing relationship                     |
| **SOW/WO** | Statement of Work/Work Order — specific deliverables for a single engagement under an MSA        |
| **NDA**    | Non-Disclosure Agreement — contractual confidentiality obligation, signed before reviewing terms |
| **BPA**    | Business Partners Agreement — formal partnership terms including ownership and decisions         |

I got confused about the difference between BPA and MSA and hopefully this clears things up for whoever is reading too!

| |BPA|MSA|
|---|---|---|
|Focus|Partnership terms and roles|Standard terms for ongoing services|
|Scope|Defines the relationship|Covers multiple future transactions|
|Detail level|High level|More detailed, legally binding|
|Common in|Joint ventures, profit sharing|IT services, vendor contracts|

# Summary
This chapter handles the business aspects with the vendor so that we can align and have a sense of security while contracting work with vendors without compromising the safety of the organisation.
